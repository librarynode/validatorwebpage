Semantic Alpha

Markets are driven by narratives, context, and information asymmetry — not just numbers. The Journal Node captures this edge by applying large language models to extract actionable trading signals from unstructured data: earnings calls, filings, social commentary, and macro research.

This is Semantic Alpha — the thesis that persistent, compounding returns come from systematically processing the meaning behind market information faster and more consistently than any human can. The Journal Node operationalizes this thesis into a continuous analytical pipeline.

Four Analytical Modes

Mode A — Macro Research

Synthesizes top-down macro indicators, central bank signals, and cross-asset flows into a directional market thesis. Output: sector and factor tilts with conviction scores.

Mode B — Earnings & Filings

Parses earnings transcripts, 10-K/10-Q filings, and guidance revisions to surface mispriced fundamentals. Output: per-ticker sentiment deltas and estimate drift signals.

Mode C — Social Sentiment

Monitors Twitter/X, Reddit, and Discord for shifts in crowd positioning and narrative momentum. Output: sentiment velocity scores and contrarian alert flags.

Mode D — Technical Context

Applies LLM-interpreted pattern recognition over price action, volume profiles, and volatility structure. Output: regime labels and key level annotations for trade framing.

PFT Token Utility

The PFT token is the native unit of coordination across the Post Fiat network. Within the Journal Node, it serves several functions:

  • Transaction Fees — PFT is consumed to anchor trade tickets and analytical outputs on-chain.
  • Minting — New PFT is minted as rewards for validated, high-quality analytical contributions to the network.
  • Staking — Node operators stake PFT to signal commitment and earn priority access to network resources.
  • Access Gating — Certain advanced modes and historical data queries require a minimum PFT balance.
